OVERVIEW

This project analyzes and categorizes the emotional language in the Bibical books of Psalms and Revelations. The types of emotional language present in both of these books are then compared in order to assess the difference in tone between the Old and New Testaments.

Our research questions:

  • What is the emotional tone and language of the book of Psalms and the book of Revelations?
  • Is there any variation from the Old Testament to the New Testament?

ABOUT THE DEVELOPERS

This project was developed in the spring 2022 semester for Computational Methods in the Digital Humanities course at the University of Pittsburgh. The team consisted of Feral Holt, Caelin Grambau, Frances Harrington and a project mentor.

  • Feral Holt is a major in Religious Studies

ABOUT

OLD AND NEW TESTAMENT

This project utilizes one book from the Old Testament and one book from the New Testament. The Old Testament consists of the first 46 books of the Christian Bible. This project uses Psalms which is a book of hymns and laments to God. The New Testament consists of 27 books and is the second half of the Christian Bible. This project uses the Book of Revelation, which is the final book of the New Testament. It outlines an apocalyptic prophecy.

METHODOLOGY

Our methodology involves tags with both elements and attributes, if you would like a more explicit understanding of what specifically we chose to tag and why, you can refer to our markup guidelines on our GitHub. We will be tagging words and phrases seen throughout the books of Revelations and Psalms in the Bible. The phrases and words that will be tagged will be specific to those that deal with emotional language. An example of this would be something like the phrase, "God, you have rejected us."

The emotions that are listed as attributes are:

  • joy
  • sorrow
  • anger
  • contentment
  • fear

The tones that are listed as attributes are:

  • pleading
  • petitioning
  • violence
  • boasting
  • anxiety
  • praise
  • fear
  • righteousness
